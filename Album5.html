Album Appraisal

This record sits deep in Rizzio’s expansion phase, where persona overtakes structure and excess becomes the method rather than the byproduct. The sequencing is deliberately chaotic, looping motifs like “Cummin’ Round Her Mountain” and “Aroundaway” into a self-referential maze that reinforces the mythology of movement, appetite, and repetition. Tracks like “Wanton Streak” and “Vitamin F” push the Freak of the Week doctrine to its rawest edge, while “Nothing Has Changed” anchors the project with ideological continuity beneath the chaos. The recurring game show host, Dirty Old Man, and Sir Rizz personas blur into one unstable narrator, suggesting identity as performance rather than truth. By the time “Here To A Special No Thank My Part Five” closes, the album feels less like a collection and more like a broadcast loop—Channel Zero fully realised, transmitting indulgence as doctrine.
