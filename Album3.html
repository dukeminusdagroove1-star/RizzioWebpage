Album Appraisal

Rizzio pushes further into confrontation here, sharpening the social blade first swung on ASK YO’ MOMMA and made more chaotic on DON’T LOOK – BUY!!. The album splits cleanly between raw street provocation and structured thematic intent—Fear Of A Black Tenant and Keep It Dark carry a heavier ideological weight, while Motorway Man and 69 double down on character-driven excess. There’s a growing self-awareness too, with Placebo Pimp Hustlin’ Gangster exposing dual-identity performance, hinting at the wider persona architecture forming. The “Here To A Special No Thank Me” thread now feels embedded canon, not novelty. Sonically and lyrically, this is less scattered than DON’T LOOK – BUY!!, but more volatile—Rizzio tightening control while expanding his world.
